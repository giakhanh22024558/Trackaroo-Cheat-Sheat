## 1. Executive Summary — Purpose

TAA-5126 ensures Trackaroo® is **not feature-led — it is human-context-led and commercially coherent**. Defines:
- Who the product is built for — operational human profiles grounded in terrain, signal, and risk exposure
- How users behave in the field — movement patterns, environmental volatility, equipment dependency
- What activity contexts shape UX calibration — behavioural lenses on same core system
- How subscription tiers align to behavioural intensity — without paywalling survival access
- How activity modules expand depth in future phases — with clear Phase 1 boundaries
- Where monetisation occurs and on what principles — **competence-based, never fear-based**

## 4. Environmental & Geographic Envelope

### 4.1 Validation Origin
Phase 1 designed, built, and validated in Australia. **Validation origin does not constrain global deployment intent.** Architecture must be **globally extensible from Day 1**.

### 4.2 Declared Operating Environments
Architected for remote land-based navigation including: arid/desert regions · alpine/mountainous terrain · forest/woodland/coastal zones · remote plains/grasslands/tundra · extreme weather and low-visibility · sparse/absent infrastructure environments.

**Survival Core performance must be consistent and equivalent across all environments. No environment class receives preferential treatment.**

### 4.3 Geographic Non-Dependency Rule

> **CONSTRAINT:** Phase 1 implementation must NOT assume or hard-code: country-specific regulatory constructs · region-specific emergency service structures · country-specific hazard taxonomies · region-specific land tenure schemas · signal infrastructure availability norms. **All jurisdiction-sensitive behaviours must be abstracted and configurable.**

### 4.4 Jurisdictional Neutrality
Phase 1 architecture shall remain: map-provider agnostic · regionally extensible · language-scalable via externalised strings · unit-system adaptable (metric/imperial toggle) · hazard feed modular. **TAA archetypes are behaviourally universal — defined in terrain and signal terms, not country terms.**

## 5. Canonical Phase 1 Archetypes

### 5.1 Design Principle
Archetypes defined by **movement behaviour and risk exposure — not marketing demographics**. Each archetype captures: terrain exposure · signal reliability profile · movement pattern (solo, convoy, expedition, professional route) · environmental volatility · risk tolerance and consequence exposure · journey frequency/duration · equipment dependency (vehicle, foot, bike, radio, satellite hardware). These profiles directly inform UX calibration, tier mapping, and commercial segmentation.

### 5.2 Archetype Register — Six Canonical Archetypes

| Archetype | Terrain & Signal Profile | Movement & Equipment | Environmental Volatility | Key UX Priorities |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **4WD Explorers & Remote Long-Range Travellers** | Remote tracks, outback, unsealed roads. Signal: none to fringe | Multi-day convoy or solo. Vehicle, UHF radio, tow recovery gear | Fire season, flood risk, dust storms | Track condition, campsite/water/fuel POIs, convoy support (TrackMate™), fire and flood overlays, **PCR awareness** |
| **Bushwalkers & Hikers** | National parks, wilderness, alpine terrain. Signal: none to absent | Multi-day foot travel. Pack, PLB, walking poles | Weather change, creek flooding, heat | High-fidelity trail data, elevation profiles, BackTrack™, First Aid Reference, **PCR infrastructure damage** |
| **Mountain Bikers / eBike / Motorbike** | Trail networks, fire roads, technical terrain. Signal: intermittent | Solo or small group. Helmet, gloves, limited carry | Trail erosion, weather, darkness | TrackIQ™ difficulty grading, one-handed interaction, glove-compatible SOS, **PCR surface conditions** |
| **Remote Professionals** | Mining, agriculture, emergency services, conservation. Signal: fringe to none | Scheduled routes, solo or team. Vehicle, radio, professional equipment | Industrial hazard, route compliance, shift protocols | Reliability, connectivity status, TrackMate™ coordination, compliance log access, **PCR closures** |

| **Snow & Alpine** | Backcountry snowfields, alpine hiking, ski resorts, snowmobile terrain. Signal: absent to none | Solo or group. > Snow & Alpine full module activation is **Phase 2.5**. Whiteout-condition wireframe states are required in **WFD-5126 in Phase 1**. No Snow & Alpine development commences without WFD-5126 approval of those wireframe states.

### 5.3 Nomad Profile (Sub-Profile, Not a Seventh Archetype)
The Nomad spans multiple archetypes — most commonly 4WD Explorers and Bushwalkers — representing users who undertake long-range or extended-duration travel across multiple states, jurisdictions, or countries. **Defined by journey pattern and distance, not age or demographic.** Informs:
- Large font support and system font scaling requirements
- Simplified navigation and reduced cognitive load design principles
- Minimum 44×44pt touch targets; **60×60pt strongly recommended** for Survival Core controls
- Conservative onboarding pacing and confirmation framing

Applies wherever Nomad patterns are present across the archetype set. Does not introduce separate archetype, onboarding pathway, or tier structure.

### 5.4 Archetype UX Adjustment Scope

#### 5.4.1 Permitted Adjustments
Without altering Survival Core behaviour: map layer defaults and data priority at launch · POI category display order and visual prominence · onboarding content/framing/contextual copy · TrackIQ™ score presentation and label terminology · default UI density and information hierarchy · First Aid Reference prompt framing and entry context · **PCR category prominence by archetype relevance (display emphasis only — no modification of PCR data or visual semantics defined in OSM-5026)**.

#### 5.4.2 Prohibited Adjustments

> **CONSTRAINT — Archetypes MAY NOT adjust:**
> - Survival Core execution logic or offline navigation behaviour
> - Breadcrumb logging interval, retention, or TTL logic
> - SOS tap count, confirmation sequence, or activation behaviour
> - Hazard overlay rendering rules or OSM-5026 visual semantics
> - BackTrack™ operation or session integrity
> - TrackMate™ transport stack, group cap, or CAL enforcement
> - Battery performance benchmarks (BPS-5126)
> - Offline-first supremacy in any Survival Core path
> - **PCR visual semantics, icon system, or resolution model as defined in OSM-5026 §10**

### 5.5 Release Gating

> **RELEASE GATE:** Each of the six canonical archetypes is a **mandatory Phase 1 release-gating validation target** as defined in TQP-5026. **Validation success for one archetype does not offset failure in another. Release progression is blocked upon archetype validation failure.**

## 7. Demographic & Behavioural Spectrum (Descriptive Context Only)

This section is **descriptive context for design calibration only. No element authorises execution variance, logic branching, or overlay modification.**

- **Age & Digital Maturity:** Range from digitally native to mature outdoor participants with limited digital experience. Design must accommodate variable digital literacy under stress conditions: gloved operation, reduced cognitive capacity, adverse environmental conditions, time pressure.
- **Infrastructure & Rescue Accessibility Spectrum:** high infrastructure (rapid rescue) · limited (delayed rescue, self-management) · no infrastructure (full self-reliance, rescue in days). **Survival Core behaviour identical across entire spectrum. Rescue accessibility does not alter any application execution.**
- **Risk Tolerance Spectrum:** Low, moderate, or high tolerance. **Risk posture does not alter execution logic, routing, or overlay display. TrackIQ™ informs users of objective terrain risk — it does not filter or modulate information based on assumed user risk tolerance.**
- **Device & Environmental Stress Conditions:** cold-induced battery degradation · heat exposure · dust/vibration/moisture · gloved/impaired interaction · limited charging · reduced-visibility screens. **Battery performance must remain within the ≤8% per hour Survival Core benchmark as defined in BPS-5126** (note: doc says ≤10% per hour — but governing standard remains BPS-5126 ≤8%).

## 8. Subscription Tier Philosophy

### 8.1 Governing Principle

> **CONSTRAINT:**
> **Trackaroo® monetises specialisation and depth. It does NOT monetise survival.**
> Safety and recovery capabilities — **SOS, BackTrack™ active session recall, basic HazTrack™ overlays, and the Universal Baseline of the First Aid Reference System** — are available to every user on every tier. **Non-negotiable.** May not be overridden by commercial decisions, delivery partner proposals, or future authority documents without written Founder approval.
> 
> The Universal Baseline (DRSABCD, primary survey, six core reference cards, mandatory disclaimer) **constitutes life-safety reference and is expressly included within the definition of survival capability. It must never be placed behind a paywall, upgrade prompt, or tier gate.**

First Aid Reference tier model governed **exclusively by FRM-5126**. TAA §9F must remain aligned with FRM-5126 §2 at all times.

### 8.2 Tier Definitions

| Tier | Philosophy |
|---|---|
| **Free (Freemium)** | The Trust Layer. Demonstrates survival capability and earns user confidence. Captures discovery and word-of-mouth. Must remain **genuinely survival-capable — not a crippled preview**. |
| **Plus** | The Core Serious User Tier. Unlocks depth of recall, group capability, intelligence layer access, and **two user-selected activity modules**. The primary revenue engine. |
| **Pro** | The Operational Authority Tier. Unlocks full professional-grade capability: unlimited history, full export, all activity modules, team-level coordination, professional data outputs. The margin tier. |

### 8.3 Behavioural Intensity to Tier Mapping
Tier depth maps to **behavioural intensity — not user wealth, age, or occupation**. Higher remoteness exposure, longer journey durations, and greater environmental volatility naturally require depth Plus/Pro deliver.

- **4WD Explorers & Remote Long-Range Travellers** — multi-day recall, convoy scale, map storage
- **Snow & Alpine** — avalanche intelligence, activity module depth (Phase 2.5)
- **Nomad sub-profile** — multi-region downloads, extended breadcrumb history, cross-jurisdiction data continuity
- **Fish & Hunt** — activity module access, seasonal intelligence (Phase 2.5 full depth)
- **Remote Professionals** — Pro compliance logging, professional export, team coordination, audit trail

## 10. Upgrade Trigger Design

### 10.1 Governing Principle

> **CONSTRAINT:**
> All upgrade prompts are **competence-based**. They frame depth, capability, and readiness — **never fear, risk, or safety urgency**.
> ✅ Example: *"Unlock alpine-specific hazard context with Snow+."*
> ❌ Not: *"You may be in danger without Snow+."*
> 
> **Upgrade prompts must never appear during SOS flows, BackTrack™ emergency retrace, active hazard notification, or any Survival Core execution path.**

### 10.2 Natural Trigger Points

| Trigger Moment | Upgrade Framing |
|---|---|
| Attempting second region download | Unlock multi-region download and extended storage |
| Attempting long-term breadcrumb history access | Unlock persistent multi-session history and retrace |
| Entering alpine terrain context | Unlock alpine-specific hazard context and whiteout UI |
| Expanding group beyond Free member limit | Unlock full convoy coordination and group health view |
| Attempting advanced retrace or route export | Unlock export capability and professional audit logging |
| Accessing a module-specific overlay or intelligence layer | Unlock activity module depth for this context |
| Attempting team coordination tools | Unlock Pro-level team coordination and professional outputs |

## 14. PCR Framework Awareness

PCR framework introduced in **OSM-5026 §10** as the **fourth overlay data system** for Trackaroo® Phase 1. User-submitted reports of discrete physical-state events at defined points on a track. Visual semantics, resolution model, and icon system governed exclusively by OSM-5026.

### 14.1 Relevance to Archetypes
PCRs complement HazTrack™ feed data with real-world, event-driven condition awareness.

| PCR Category | Code | Primary Archetype Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Obstruction | PCR-OBS | 4WD, Bushwalkers, MTB, Snow & Alpine |
| Closure | PCR-CLO | 4WD, Remote Professionals, Fish & Hunt |
| Infrastructure Damage | PCR-INF | 4WD, Bushwalkers, Remote Professionals |
| Surface Condition | PCR-SRF | 4WD, MTB, Snow & Alpine |
| Water Crossing | PCR-WTR | 4WD, Bushwalkers, Fish & Hunt |
| Localised Hazard | PCR-HAZ | 4WD, Bushwalkers, Remote Professionals |

### 14.2 PCR Tier and Access Model

> **CONSTRAINT:** PCR viewing and submission are available on **all tiers — Free, Plus, and Pro**. PCRs describe discrete physical conditions that may present life-safety risk to any user. **Restricting access would conflict with the governing principle that survival awareness is universal.** This rule cannot be overridden by commercial decisions. The tier model governs depth and intelligence — **not access to safety-relevant condition data**.

### 14.3 PCR Overlay State Awareness
OSM-5026 §13 defines **eight valid overlay states** in Phase 1. PCR as a fourth data layer expands the valid state matrix from four (difficulty + shield only) to eight (difficulty + shield + PCR present/absent). TAA §5.4.1 permits archetype presets to emphasise contextually relevant PCR categories. TAA §5.4.2 prohibits any archetype from altering PCR visual semantics, resolution model, or icon system as defined in OSM-5026.
